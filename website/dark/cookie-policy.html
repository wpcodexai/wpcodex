Cookie Policy

What cookies WPCodex uses and how you can control them.

Last updated: June 13, 2026

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They serve different purposes — keeping you logged in, remembering your preferences, and helping website owners understand how their site is used.

Cookies we use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
wpcodex_session Essential Maintains your logged-in session on the WPCodex account portal. Session
wpcodex_csrf Essential Cross-site request forgery protection token. Session
wpcodex_prefs Functional Stores your cookie banner preference (accepted/declined). 1 year
_analytics_id Analytics Anonymous visitor identifier for our privacy-respecting analytics tool. No cross-site tracking. IP is not stored. 6 months

Essential cookies

Essential cookies are strictly necessary for the website to function. They cannot be disabled because the site wouldn't work without them (e.g., staying logged in to the account portal, CSRF protection). They do not track you across other websites.

Functional cookies

Functional cookies remember choices you make, such as whether you've accepted the cookie banner. They improve your experience but are not strictly necessary.

Analytics cookies

We use a privacy-respecting, cookieless analytics tool to understand how visitors use our website — which pages are popular, where visitors come from, and how long they stay. Our analytics setup:

  • Does not track you across other websites
  • Does not store your IP address
  • Does not build individual profiles
  • Aggregates data at the cohort level
  • Is compliant with GDPR and ePrivacy without requiring consent in most jurisdictions

Third-party cookies

We embed YouTube videos on our Videos page. YouTube may set cookies when you play a video. We use YouTube's privacy-enhanced mode (youtube-nocookie.com) to minimise cookie use, but we cannot fully control YouTube's cookie behaviour once you interact with their player.

How to control cookies

You can manage cookies in several ways:

  • Cookie banner: On your first visit, choose your preferences in the banner that appears at the bottom of the screen.
  • Browser settings: Most browsers let you view, delete, and block cookies. See your browser's help pages for instructions.
  • Opt-out tools: For analytics, you can use browser-level Do Not Track signals — we honour these.

Note: blocking essential cookies will prevent login functionality from working correctly.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy if we add new cookies or change our analytics tools. Material changes will be communicated via the cookie banner or a notice on this page.
